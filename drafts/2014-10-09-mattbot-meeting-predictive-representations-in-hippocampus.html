Mattbot Meeting: Predictive Representations in Hippocampus

Background

Successor Representation + predictive representations in cortex and hippocampus (Anna’s paper) –> let’s look at place cells

Model Assumptions

Receptive field of place fields is predictive of future locations under a transition policy

Eigendecomposition of place cells yields grid cells (take eigendecomposition of successor representation)

Simulated Place and Grid Cells

Place Cells: Halo around particular locations that dies off as increase distance from center, because you’re less likely to visit those - this makes sense!
Grid Cells: Observe distinct frequencies along x and y-axis which Kim didn’t think actually happened in grid cells, but Michael Yaervitsch said there are in fact some papers that show that

Things that are unique to predictive model

Direction Selectivity: if rat starts traveling in one direction, then place cells change firing rate accordingly to be skewed in direction of reward

Skewing Towards Reward: see same thing, how do we know whether it’s just movement or actual reward that the hippocampus cares about because reward dictates policy i.e. movement
(Michael Hasselmo has a paper; also, Accumulation of Hippocampal Place Fields at the Goal Location in an Annular Watermaze Task)

Obstacle Avoidance: if reward is behind obstacle, place cells skew around obstacle too - again, for same reasons as last three

Hairpin Maze: physiological results show grid cells compartmentalize within columns of hairpin maze AND direction-selective, Kim’s results compartmentalize along columns but aren’t directionally-selective; Some people think you get directional selectivity across columns in rats because maybe there is a resetting at corners

Perhaps the issue here is that you get different views when you go in different directions, you’re getting different views and actually successor representation should have richer representation of state that takes into account VIEW as states, not just tabular, abstract state representation

Spectral Segmentation

Successor representation gives you the same matrix as the graph laplacian, which can be used to do spectral segmentation (use approximate normalized graph cuts) for “community detection” so perhaps that’s a function of the hippocampal representations: useful for segmentation
